    MemoryOS v0.95 — Choice-Free Signature Hull and Kernel-Order Duality¶

    Status¶

    MemoryOS v0.95 advances the v0.94 choice-free minimal-generator antichain signature from an equality classifier to an exact order representation.

    The complete antichain remains intact. No generator is selected as a preferred representative.

    Source frontier¶

    Choice-free envelope and closed hull¶

    For a finite antichain signature A, define its envelope by

    Env(A) = union of every support M in A.

    Define its closed hull by

    Hull_r(A) = cl_r(Env(A)).

    For a proper closed context C, v0.94 supplies the complete signature

    Sig_r(C) = { M | M is closure-minimal and cl_r(M) = C }.

    MemoryOS v0.95 proves

    Hull_r(Sig_r(C)) = C.

    The envelope itself need not equal C. For example, a context may be generated by the empty support. Closure of the complete envelope is the exact reconstruction invariant.

    Exact kernel preservation¶

    For a support S whose closure is proper, define its closure-class signature

    ClassSig_r(S) = Sig_r(cl_r(S)).

    Then

    Hull_r(ClassSig_r(S)) = cl_r(S)

    and

    K_r^(Env(ClassSig_r(S))) = K_r^S.

    Thus the full choice-free signature can be collapsed to one envelope for reconstruction without changing the exact invisible-kernel class. This does not choose one minimal generator.

    Signature-hull order¶

    For antichain signatures A and B, define

    A <=_Hull B  iff  Hull_r(A) subset Hull_r(B).

    For proper closed contexts C,D, the Lean layer proves

    Sig_r(C) <=_Hull Sig_r(D)
  iff C subset D.

    Closed-context inclusion is exactly reverse invisible-kernel inclusion:

    C subset D
  iff K_r^D <= K_r^C.

    Therefore

    Sig_r(C) <=_Hull Sig_r(D)
  iff K_r^D <= K_r^C.

    For proper support closure classes S,T,

    ClassSig_r(S) <=_Hull ClassSig_r(T)
  iff cl_r(S) subset cl_r(T)
  iff K_r^T <= K_r^S.

    Antisymmetry¶

    If two proper closed-context signatures are mutually below each other in hull order, their represented contexts are equal and the signatures themselves are equal.

    Sig_r(C) <=_Hull Sig_r(D)
and Sig_r(D) <=_Hull Sig_r(C)
implies Sig_r(C) = Sig_r(D).

    This upgrades v0.94 from a choice-free equality invariant to a choice-free partial-order representation of the proper closure quotient.

    Root independence¶

    The following are independent of the selected route root:

    • context-signature envelopes
    • context-signature closed hulls
    • closure-class-signature envelopes
    • closure-class-signature closed hulls

    The order representation therefore does not depend on root-chart presentation.

    Literature alignment¶

    The immediate knowledge-expansion context remains the 2026 work on retrieval-grounded Formal Concept Analysis and inspectable counterexamples (arXiv:2607.01773).

    The principal new representation reference is Poncet, Order-generation in posets and convolution of closure operators (arXiv:2509.05299, revised March 2026). That work studies reconstruction of ordered objects from closure-theoretic generating points and finite antichains in the finitary case. MemoryOS imports only the finite reconstruction and order-generation pattern.

    The conversion boundary remains aligned with work on representations of finite closure systems and hypergraph dualization (arXiv:2506.24052, arXiv:2407.00694). No complexity result from those papers is claimed here.
