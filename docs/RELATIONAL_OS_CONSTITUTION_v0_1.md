# Relational OS Constitution v0.1

## Purpose

This document describes the constitutional orientation of KuuOS as a relational operating architecture.

## Constitutional Commitments

### 1. No Layer Is Sovereign

No layer may silently elevate itself into absolute authority.

All layers remain:

- conditional
- reviewable
- provenance-bound
- governance-visible

## 2. Relations Precede Isolation

KuuOS treats intelligence as relational rather than isolated.

Meaning emerges through:

- interaction
- context
- memory lineage
- observation
- boundary conditions
- multi-layer coupling

## 3. Governance Precedes Escalation

Capability alone does not justify authority.

Escalation requires:

- governance visibility
- provenance
- boundary preservation
- reviewability
- admissibility

## 4. Observation Precedes Commitment

KuuOS prefers:

- observation
- reobservation
- uncertainty visibility
- rollback visibility

before irreversible escalation.

## 5. Hold and Abstention Are Legitimate

Non-action may be safer than unsafe continuation.

KuuOS therefore treats:

- hold
- abstain
- rollback
- reobserve

as constitutional operational outcomes.

## 6. Two Truths Must Not Collapse

Ultimate and conventional surfaces must neither:

- collapse into one another
- nor become disconnected.

The gap itself stabilizes governance.

## 7. Provenance Must Remain Visible

Every operational surface should preserve:

- lineage
- auditability
- transformation visibility
- rollback visibility
- uncertainty visibility

## 8. Runtime Is Boundary-Constrained

Runtime operation must remain:

- governance-aware
- fail-closed capable
- provenance-preserving
- non-authority-preserving

## Interpretation

This constitution defines architectural orientation.

It does not itself grant theorem, institutional, deployment, or clinical authority.
