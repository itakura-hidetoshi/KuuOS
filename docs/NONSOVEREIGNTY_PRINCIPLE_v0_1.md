# Nonsovereignty Principle v0.1

## Purpose

This document defines the nonsovereignty principle inside KuuOS.

## Core Principle

No operational layer is inherently sovereign.

No layer may silently elevate itself into:

- ultimate authority
- irreversible execution authority
- unquestionable truth status
- universal governance center

## Why This Matters

Without nonsovereignty constraints:

- models become dogmatic
- memory becomes absolutized
- validation becomes proof
- runtime becomes unchecked escalation
- coordination becomes domination

## Governance Consequence

KuuOS therefore preserves:

- reviewability
- rollback capability
- uncertainty visibility
- provenance visibility
- bounded authority
- conditional admissibility

## Runtime Consequence

Operational authority remains:

- scoped
- conditional
- governance-visible
- reversible where possible

## Relation to Mandala

Mandala coexistence depends on preventing any single operational world from silently totalizing all others.

## Relation to Two Truths Gap

The Two Truths Gap prevents operational surfaces from collapsing into ultimate sovereign authority.

## Interpretation

This principle defines governance orientation.

It does not itself grant theorem, institutional, deployment, or clinical authority.
