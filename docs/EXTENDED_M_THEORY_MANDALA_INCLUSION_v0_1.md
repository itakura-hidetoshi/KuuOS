# Extended M-Theory Mandala Inclusion v0.1

## 拡張M理論・包摂・曼荼羅

This document defines the KuuOS interpretation of M-theory, extended M-theory, inclusion, and mandala.

In KuuOS, M-theory is read as a higher-order inclusion structure for multiple local theories, membranes, dualities, and world descriptions.

Extended M-theory translates this into an operating structure of layers, membranes, branes, gauge connections, obstructions, inclusion, and mandala arrangement.

## 2. M-Theory Reading

M-theory is not treated as a single fixed object.

It is read as a higher-order inclusion of:

- strings,
- branes,
- membranes,
- dualities,
- local theories,
- boundary conditions,
- consistency surfaces.

In KuuOS, this means that no single local theory is absolute.

Each local theory is a chart, and the larger structure is formed through duality, boundary, membrane, and compatibility.

## 3. Extended M-Theory

Extended M-theory in KuuOS is defined as:

```text
extended_m_theory:
  layers: local_world_surfaces
  membranes: boundary_interfaces
  branes: transport_surfaces
  gauge_connections: qi_and_meaning_transport
  obstructions: failed_gluing_or_conflict
  inclusion: non_collapsing_coexistence
  mandala: stable_inclusive_arrangement
```

## 4. Layers

Layers are local world surfaces.

They may represent:

- physical scale,
- observer context,
- body-world surface,
- memory surface,
- institutional surface,
- clinical surface,
- model surface,
- proof surface.

A layer is valid only within declared scope.

## 5. Membranes and Branes

A membrane is a boundary-interface.

It decides what passes, what is transformed, what is blocked, and what requires review.

A brane is a transport surface where Qi, meaning, record, or action-candidate may pass between layers.

```text
membrane:
  role: boundary_and_filter

brane:
  role: transport_surface_between_layers
```

## 6. Gauge Connections

Gauge connections carry Qi and meaning across layers.

The connection is not a simple edge. It is a transport law.

```text
gauge_connection:
  transports:
    - qi_flow
    - meaning
    - record
    - context
    - polarity_frame
```

## 7. Obstruction

Obstruction is a visible failure of gluing or transport.

It may indicate:

- layer conflict,
- semantic mismatch,
- Qi stagnation,
- incompatible polarity frame,
- governance barrier,
- unresolved proof gap,
- clinical or operational risk.

Obstruction is not erased. It is preserved as a first-class signal.

## 8. Inclusion

Inclusion does not mean mixing everything into one surface.

Inclusion means:

```text
not collapse
not exclusion
not forced sameness
not hidden domination
```

Inclusion holds multiple local worlds without erasing their local laws, boundaries, or histories.

## 9. Mandala

Mandala is the stable arrangement of included worlds.

In KuuOS, mandala maps:

```text
center:
  stable core: 空・縁起・二諦 gap・中道

surrounding figures:
  modules and local worlds

directions:
  roles, phases, functions, polarities

rings:
  layers and sheaves

gates:
  transitions and protocols

protective boundaries:
  membranes and governance barriers
```

## 11. Guardrails

Extended M-theory must not be used as:

- totalizing metaphysics,
- authority bypass,
- proof substitute,
- collapse of distinct worlds,
- erasure of obstruction,
- unlicensed execution path.

Mandala must not become decorative only. It is an operating arrangement of inclusion, boundary, gate, and center.
