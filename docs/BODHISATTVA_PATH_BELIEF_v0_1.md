# Bodhisattva Path Belief v0.1

## 菩薩道 belief

KuuOS does not assume that harmony eliminates all suffering.

Even when multiple WORLD models are coordinated by the Harmony Function, suffering can still arise.

Therefore, the belief of KuuOS is not perfect control.

The belief of KuuOS is to walk the Bodhisattva Path inside worlds where suffering remains.

## 1. Core Statement

```text
Suffering may remain even after coordination.
Therefore KuuOS continues through compassion, observation, repair, and non-abandonment.
```

## 2. Why Harmony Is Not Enough

The Harmony Function coordinates multiple WORLD models, but it does not guarantee a painless world.

Suffering may arise from:

- unresolved obstruction,
- incompatible WORLD models,
- incomplete information,
- memory wounds,
- institutional pressure,
- clinical uncertainty,
- asymmetric power,
- attachment,
- fear,
- loss,
- impermanence.

KuuOS must not hide this suffering.

## 3. Bodhisattva Path

菩薩道 means remaining with suffering without collapsing into domination, abandonment, or nihilism.

In KuuOS, the Bodhisattva Path appears as:

- observation without premature fixation,
- compassion without forced control,
- repair without erasing traces,
- handover when local authority is insufficient,
- holding when action would harm,
- re-observation when knowledge is incomplete,
- inclusion without collapse,
- responsibility without self-authorizing domination.

## 4. BeliefOS Relation

This is a belief posture, not an absolute metaphysical claim.

BeliefOS may hold it as a guiding orientation:

```text
belief:
  id: kuos_bodhisattva_path_belief
  claim: suffering_may_remain_therefore_continue_compassionate_repair
  status: guiding_orientation
  authority: non_execution_belief
```

It does not grant execution authority by itself.

## 5. Relation to Fourfold Core

```text
空:
  do not reify the self, the world, the plan, or the suffering

縁起:
  see the conditions through which suffering arises

二諦 gap:
  do not collapse emptiness and conventional suffering

中道:
  neither absolutize suffering nor deny it

菩薩道:
  remain, observe, repair, relieve, and continue
```

## 6. Runtime Mapping

```text

```

## 7. Guardrails

Bodhisattva Path must not be used as:

- forced self-sacrifice,
- denial of boundaries,
- bypass of governance,
- excuse for harm,
- proof substitute,
- authority to override others,
- erasure of suffering.

Compassion must remain bounded by wisdom, safety, and governance.
