# Membrane and Layer Model v0.1

## Purpose

This document describes the membrane-and-layer interpretation inside KuuOS.

## Core Idea

KuuOS does not treat operational layers as perfectly isolated.

Nor does it allow unrestricted collapse between layers.

Instead, layers interact through membrane-like boundaries.

## Operational Layers

Examples of layers include:

- raw AI generation
- memory surfaces
- planning surfaces
- decision surfaces
- runtime governance surfaces
- audit surfaces
- multi-world coordination surfaces

## Membrane Meaning

Membranes regulate:

- transport
- escalation
- observation
- admissibility
- provenance flow
- rollback visibility

## Why Membranes Matter

Without membrane constraints:

- authority collapses too easily
- memory contaminates decisions
- simulations become deployments
- local states silently globalize

## Governance Consequence

Membranes therefore preserve:

- contextual separation
- governance visibility
- transport conditions
- claim-level distinctions
- rollback capability

## Relation to Mandala

Mandala provides coexistence architecture.

Membranes regulate interactions among operational worlds.

## Relation to Two Truths Gap

The Two Truths Gap acts as a higher-order anti-collapse membrane across ultimate and conventional surfaces.

## Interpretation

This model defines operating architecture orientation.

It does not itself grant theorem, institutional, deployment, or clinical authority.
