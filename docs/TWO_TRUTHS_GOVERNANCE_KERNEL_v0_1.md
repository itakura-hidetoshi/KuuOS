# Two Truths Governance Kernel v0.1

## Purpose

This document explains the role of the Two Truths Gap as a governance stabilizer in KuuOS.

## Core Structure

KuuOS distinguishes:

- 勝義諦 / ultimate-facing surfaces
- 世俗諦 / operational-conventional surfaces

These are neither identical nor disconnected.

The gap itself is operationally important.

## Governance Meaning

Without the gap:

- abstractions collapse into operational absolutism
- operational surfaces claim ultimate authority
- validation collapses into proof
- memory collapses into truth
- simulation collapses into deployment

## Gap as Stabilizer

The Two Truths Gap prevents:

- silent authority escalation
- reification
- governance collapse
- total flattening of layers

## Runtime Interpretation

Operational systems live primarily on the conventional side.

But conventional operation must remain constrained by:

- emptiness
- relational dependence
- boundary awareness
- non-sovereignty

## Architectural Consequence

KuuOS therefore preserves:

- claim-level separation
- provenance visibility
- governance routing
- rollback visibility
- abstention legitimacy

rather than allowing direct collapse into execution authority.

## Mandala Relation

The Two Truths Gap enables Mandala-like coexistence of multiple operational worlds without forced flattening into a single sovereign frame.

## Interpretation

This kernel defines governance orientation.

It does not itself grant theorem, institutional, deployment, or clinical authority.
