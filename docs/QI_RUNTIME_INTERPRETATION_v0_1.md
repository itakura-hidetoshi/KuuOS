# Qi Runtime Interpretation v0.1

## Purpose

This document describes the operational interpretation of Qi inside KuuOS runtime architecture.

## Core Clarification

Qi is not treated merely as metaphor.

Inside KuuOS, Qi refers to effective relational flow surfaces operating primarily on the conventional side of the Two Truths Gap.

## Operational Meaning

Qi may represent:

- relational momentum
- coordination flow
- memory activation tendency
- planning pressure
- attention concentration
- governance tension
- stabilization or destabilization tendency

## Runtime Role

Qi participates in:

- runtime coordination
- operational coupling
- local-global mediation
- multi-layer interaction
- transport sensitivity

## Governance Constraints

Qi does not override governance.

Qi remains constrained by:

- provenance
- admissibility
- rollback visibility
- fail-closed governance
- claim-level separation

## Failure Modes

Unregulated Qi concentration may contribute to:

- runaway escalation
- authority collapse
- coordination instability
- local-global distortion
- governance bypass

## Relation to Yin-Yang

Qi dynamics are interpreted through contextual polarity rather than fixed substance.

## Relation to Mandala

Qi participates in coordination among multiple operational worlds without requiring flattening into a single sovereign frame.

## Interpretation

This runtime interpretation defines operational orientation.

It does not itself grant theorem, institutional, deployment, or clinical authority.
