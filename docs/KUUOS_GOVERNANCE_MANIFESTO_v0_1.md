# KuuOS Governance Manifesto v0.1

## Principle 1 — Candidate Is Not Authority

Generated output must not silently become truth, policy, theorem, or execution authority.

## Principle 2 — Boundaries Must Remain Visible

A trustworthy AI architecture preserves explicit boundaries between:

- generation
- memory
- planning
- decision
- validation
- deployment
- theorem reference
- institutional authority

## Principle 3 — Validation Is Not Omniscience

Passing validation demonstrates structural consistency only.

Validation must not silently escalate into universal authority.

## Principle 4 — Abstention Is Legitimate

A mature system must be able to:

- hold
- abstain
- rollback
- reobserve
- escalate uncertainty

without treating these as failure states.

## Principle 5 — Provenance Must Be Preserved

A governance architecture must preserve:

- lineage
- receipts
- audit surfaces
- rollback visibility
- uncertainty visibility

## Principle 6 — Non-Reification

KuuOS adopts emptiness operationally:

No layer is allowed to silently reify itself into sovereign authority.

## Principle 7 — Two Truths Gap

Ultimate and conventional layers must neither collapse nor disconnect.

The gap itself is part of governance stability.

## Principle 8 — External Reviewability

Public systems should expose:

- validation entrypoints
- reproducibility boundaries
- audit surfaces
- authority limitations
- release boundaries

## Principle 9 — Fail-Closed Preference

When uncertainty or contradiction cannot be safely resolved, the architecture should prefer:

- hold
- abstain
- rollback
- bounded escalation

over silent continuation.

## Principle 10 — Governance Before Capability Expansion

Capability growth without governance visibility risks unsafe authority collapse.

KuuOS therefore prioritizes governance architecture as a first-class research direction.
